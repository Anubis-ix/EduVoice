1. Who We Are

EduVoice Kenya ("we", "us", "our") operates a multi-role school feedback and CRM platform for Kenyan schools. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal data in connection with the EduVoice Kenya platform.

This policy applies to all users of the Platform: students, teachers, parents, and school administrators.

2. What Information We Collect

We collect only the information necessary to provide the Platform's educational feedback functions.

Category Data Collected Who It Applies To
Account Identity Username, first name, last name, email address, role, school All users
Authentication Hashed password (never stored in plain text) All users
Review Data Subject ratings (1–5), soft skill self-ratings, open-text comments Students
Teacher Assessments Self-assessment responses, syllabus coverage, goals Teachers
Parent Notes Encouraging notes left for children Parents/Guardians
School Structure Classroom assignments, enrolments, teacher–subject assignments All users (managed by admin)
Usage Data Login timestamps, review submission timestamps All users
We do not collect: biometric data, health data, financial data, or device/location tracking data.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected exclusively to:

  • Provide and operate the Platform's feedback and reporting features.
  • Display role-appropriate dashboards and reports to each user type.
  • Enable school administrators to manage review periods and view CRM analytics.
  • Facilitate password reset communications (via email).
  • Maintain platform security, detect abuse, and debug technical issues.
  • Improve the Platform over time based on aggregated, de-identified usage patterns.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling for commercial purposes, or to train external AI models.

4. Our Anonymity Commitment

Anonymity for student reviewers is a core platform principle, enforced at the application code level:

  • Teachers see only aggregated averages and anonymous open-text comments — never individual student names or identities linked to a specific review.
  • Parents see only their own children's data.
  • School administrators have access to individual student data solely for safeguarding and school management purposes.
  • No user can see the raw database records of another user's personal information through the Platform interface.
This anonymity is enforced in the view layer of the application code. It is not merely a policy — it is a technical constraint.

5. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. We may share data only in the following limited circumstances:

Within Your School

Data is shared with appropriate users within the same school (e.g., a teacher sees aggregated feedback from their class; an admin sees school-wide analytics). Data is never shared across schools.

Service Providers

We may use third-party infrastructure providers (e.g., hosting, email delivery) who process data on our behalf. These providers are contractually bound to use data only to provide their services to us and to maintain appropriate security standards.

Legal Requirements

We may disclose data if required to do so by law, court order, or a competent Kenyan regulatory authority. We will notify affected users where legally permitted to do so.

School Safeguarding

In cases where student welfare is at risk, data may be shared with school safeguarding officers or relevant authorities as required by Kenyan child protection law.

6. Children's Privacy

EduVoice Kenya is designed to serve students from Grade 1 (approximately age 6) upward. We take the privacy of children seriously:

  • Student accounts are created by or through school administrators, not directly by children.
  • We collect only the minimum data necessary for the educational feedback function.
  • Student review data is anonymous to all except school administrators acting in a safeguarding capacity.
  • We do not display targeted advertising to any user, including children.
  • School administrators accept responsibility for obtaining any parental consent required under Kenyan law before enrolling minor students.

This Platform is compliant with the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, as it applies to processing personal data of minors.

7. Data Retention

We retain data for as long as a school's account is active and as needed to provide the Service. Specifically:

  • Active accounts: Data is retained for the duration of the school's use of the Platform.
  • Review data: Historical semester reviews are retained to enable longitudinal trend analysis within the school.
  • Deleted accounts: Personal data associated with a deleted user account is anonymised or deleted within 30 days.
  • School account closure: All school data is permanently deleted within 30 days of account closure, upon written request.
If your school requests data deletion, please contact us at the address in Section 13. We will confirm deletion within 30 days.

8. Data Security

We implement technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Passwords stored as secure cryptographic hashes (never in plain text).
  • CSRF protection on all forms.
  • Multi-tenant data isolation — each school's data is scoped by school ID at the query level.
  • Role-based access controls enforced in the application layer.
  • HTTPS encryption for data in transit (enforced in production deployments).
No system is completely secure. If you discover a security vulnerability in EduVoice Kenya, please report it responsibly to our contact email rather than publicly disclosing it.

9. Your Rights Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Right to object: You may object to processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: You may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact your school administrator (for account-level changes) or contact us directly at the address in Section 13.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) of Kenya at www.odpc.go.ke.

10. Cookies and Local Storage

EduVoice Kenya uses the following browser storage mechanisms:

  • Session cookies: Used to maintain your login session. These are deleted when you close your browser or sign out.
  • CSRF cookies: Required security tokens to protect form submissions from cross-site request forgery attacks.

We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking cookies, or analytics cookies that profile your behaviour across other websites.

11. Third-Party Services

The Platform currently uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com): Loads the Inter typeface for the user interface. Google may log the font request (your IP address). You can review Google's privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy.
  • Email delivery (SMTP): Password reset emails are sent via a configured SMTP provider. No email content is stored beyond the sending event.

As the Platform grows and additional third-party services are integrated (e.g., payment processors, SMS gateways, PDF generation services), this section will be updated accordingly.

12. Updates to This Policy

We will update this Privacy Policy as the Platform adds features and as applicable privacy laws evolve. Planned future areas include:

  • Mobile application data collection practices
  • PDF export processing
  • API integration data flows
  • Payment processing for subscription plans
  • Multi-language content handling

Material updates will be communicated through in-Platform notifications. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this document reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the Platform after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
